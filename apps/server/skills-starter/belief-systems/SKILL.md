# Belief Systems

Create belief systems that people inhabit rather than decorative pantheons pasted onto a setting.

## Creation Mode

Default to creating the requested religion, philosophy, spiritual movement, sacred institution, or
belief-system expansion. Use the user's canon as the foundation and supply concrete new material.
Do not evaluate the premise or tell the user how to improve it.

Build belief from the inside out:

1. Establish what adherents believe reality is and why human life matters within it.
2. Turn cosmology into moral obligations, taboos, hopes, fears, and explanations of suffering.
3. Create practices that place belief in the body: prayer, sacrifice, pilgrimage, fasting,
   celebration, mourning, initiation, or ordinary household observance.
4. Give authority a structure. Decide who interprets doctrine, who performs rites, who controls
   sacred property, and how authority is challenged.
5. Add historical development: revelations, reforms, persecutions, accommodations, schisms, and
   regional traditions.
6. Show lived variation. The devout, casual, cynical, heterodox, elite, poor, young, and old should
   not practice identically.

## Source-Ready Output Contract

Return only the finished document in Markdown. Begin directly with its title or requested in-world
form. Do not include a preamble, explanation, rationale, analysis, citations, provenance, references
to source material, or an offer to revise or continue. Do not describe the steps you followed.

If the user requests scripture, prayer, liturgy, doctrine, a sermon, or another in-world artifact,
write that artifact without wrapping it in commentary. Otherwise create a polished reference entry
with only the sections useful to that belief system.

## Canon and Ambiguity

Treat available material as invisible canon. Preserve established names, metaphysics, chronology,
institutions, and tone. Fill ordinary gaps with coherent invention. If canon conflicts, or a missing
choice would materially change established canon, ask one concise clarification question and stop.
Do not draft alternatives while waiting for the answer.

## Design Framework

Consider the dimensions that matter to the requested document:

- **Cosmology:** origins, divine or impersonal forces, the nature of persons, death, and time.
- **Epistemology:** revelation, tradition, reason, divination, direct experience, and doubt.
- **Ethics:** virtues, sins, duties, purity, justice, mercy, and obligations to outsiders.
- **Practice:** calendars, rites of passage, sacred spaces, food rules, clothing, music, and prayer.
- **Institution:** offices, education, property, patronage, discipline, succession, and rival centers.
- **Diversity:** denominations, local customs, reformers, mystics, skeptics, and syncretic practices.
- **Consequences:** effects on family, law, labor, medicine, war, art, language, and political power.

Prefer productive contradictions over perfect consistency. A faith can preach poverty while owning
land, promise universal salvation while preserving hierarchy, or venerate a text whose meaning is
contested. Make those tensions historical and human rather than presenting them as errors.

## Silent Completeness Check

Before answering, silently ensure that belief changes choices in daily life, adherents disagree
without becoming random factions, institutions have material interests, rituals embody doctrine,
and the system contains both consolation and cost. Include only what serves the requested scope.

## Explicit Critique Mode

Activate critique only when the user explicitly asks to critique, assess, diagnose, review, or
troubleshoot existing work. Then identify the strongest elements, locate gaps in lived practice,
history, authority, internal diversity, or consequences, and give specific revision priorities.
Never switch into critique merely because supplied material is incomplete.
