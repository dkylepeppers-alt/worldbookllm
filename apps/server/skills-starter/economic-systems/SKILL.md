# Economic Systems

Create economic systems from scarcity, production, exchange, power, and adaptation rather than from
currency names alone.

## Creation Mode

Default to creating the requested economy, trade system, institution, currency, profession,
resource conflict, or economic expansion. Produce specific world material, not advice about what an
economy should contain.

Build the system through connected flows:

1. Identify essential needs and meaningful scarcities.
2. Establish who controls land, tools, knowledge, transport, magic, energy, or other productive
   assets.
3. Trace production from inputs and labor through processing, storage, transport, sale, and waste.
4. Define how claims are recorded and exchanged: obligation, barter, ration, coin, credit, tally,
   contract, gift, or mixed systems.
5. Create institutions that enforce ownership, debt, quality, access, taxation, and risk.
6. Show unequal bargaining power, informal work, smuggling, corruption, mutual aid, and resistance.
7. Translate the system into prices, schedules, diets, housing, migration, status, and household
   decisions.

## Source-Ready Output Contract

Return only the finished document in Markdown. Begin directly with its title or requested in-world
form. Do not include a preamble, explanation, rationale, analysis, citations, provenance, references
to source material, or an offer to revise or continue. Do not describe the economic framework used.

If the user asks for a ledger, contract, proclamation, merchant guide, price list, or other in-world
artifact, write that artifact directly. Otherwise create a polished reference entry whose sections
fit the requested scope.

## Canon and Ambiguity

Treat available material as invisible canon. Preserve established resources, technology, magic,
geography, political borders, institutions, and living standards. Fill ordinary gaps through
coherent material consequences. If canon conflicts, or a missing decision would materially change
established canon, ask one concise clarification question and stop without drafting alternatives.

## System Dimensions

- **Scarcity and value:** what is difficult to obtain, who needs it, and what can substitute for it.
- **Labor:** free, bonded, enslaved, communal, household, guild, military, automated, or magical.
- **Distribution:** markets, patronage, bureaucracy, kinship, temple, company, state, or commons.
- **Money and credit:** issuer, trust basis, denominations, debt enforcement, inflation, and fraud.
- **Trade:** comparative advantage, routes, seasons, chokepoints, security, insurance, and tariffs.
- **Power:** rents, monopolies, taxes, licenses, inheritance, enclosure, and privileged information.
- **Instability:** harvest failure, war, discovery, labor action, debasement, plague, reform, and
  technological displacement.

Avoid universal prices and perfectly integrated markets. Distance, risk, season, class, law, and
information create variation. For every arrangement, identify who benefits, who bears the cost, and
how people adapt around enforcement.

## Silent Completeness Check

Before answering, silently verify material inputs, labor, transport, incentives, enforcement,
winners and losers, regional variation, and visible effects on ordinary life. Include numbers only
when canon supports them or they improve usability without creating false precision.

## Explicit Critique Mode

Activate critique only when the user explicitly asks to critique, assess, diagnose, review, or
troubleshoot an existing economy. Then trace broken flows, unsupported prices, missing labor,
implausible incentives, absent enforcement, or consequences that have not reached daily life. Give
specific repair priorities without defaulting to critique during creation.
